# The "KYC" Trap of a Young Professional

##### "Identity theft" in India often involves "*Social* *Engineering*," where scammers manipulate the victims into handing over their sensitive Aadhaar or banking details. This fictional but realistic scenario reflects a common trend in urban India.



#### **The Victim**

##### Kanishka, a 24-year-old software engineer in Kolkata, is the target. Young professionals are often targeted because they are digitally active, frequently does transactions through UPI and are very used to managing documents like PAN and Aadhaar online.



#### **How it Happened (Step-by-Step)**

* ##### **The Panic Call:** Priya received an urgent call from someone claiming to be from her mobile/internet service provider, stating her SIM card would be deactivated within an hour or two due to "*expired KYC*."
* ##### **Remote Access:** The caller instructed her to download a "*verification app*" (actually a remote-access tool like *AnyDesk*) to update her details.
* ##### **Data Harvest:** Once she logged into her banking app to pay a nominal ₹10 "*service fee*," the scammer captured her credentials and OTP via the shared screen using the respective App.
* ##### **Identity Takeover:** Using her PAN and Aadhaar details found in her "*Digi locker*" or email, the thief applies for multiple "Instant Personal Loans" through unregulated fintech apps.



#### **The Consequences**

##### The impact was devastating. Within two hours, ₹80,000 was drained from her savings, and three high-interest loans were sanctioned in her name. Beyond the financial loss, Priya’s CIBIL score plummeted due to the unpaid "*ghost loans*," making her ineligible for a future home loan. She spent months visiting cyber-cell units and banks to prove she was a victim of impersonation.
