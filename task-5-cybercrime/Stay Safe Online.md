### Stay Safe Online

* #### **Secure Your UPI Apps:** Set a strong, unique UPI PIN that is different from your phone lock or ATM PIN. Never share this PIN or your OTP with anyone, even if they claim to be from your bank or a government agency.
* #### **Beware of "Request Money" Scams:** Remember that you never need to enter your UPI PIN or scan a QR code to receive money (like a scholarship, refund, or OLX payment). If someone asks you to "*authorize*" a payment to your account, it is a scam.
* #### **Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Banking:** Never log into your student portal, bank account, or UPI apps using free campus or cafe Wi-Fi. Use your mobile data or a trusted VPN to prevent "*Man-in-the-Middle*" data theft.
* #### **Spot "Loan App" Traps:** Be cautious of instant-loan apps on the Play Store that ask for access to your contacts and gallery. Use only RBI-regulated NBFC apps to avoid identity theft and harassment.
* #### **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Activate 2FA on your Google, WhatsApp, and Instagram accounts. This ensures that even if a hacker gets your password, they cannot access your account without a secondary code.
* #### **Verify "KYC" Calls:** Hang up on callers claiming your SIM card or bank account will be blocked due to "*Pending KYC*". Official entities like Jio, Airtel or SBI will never ask for sensitive details over a phone call.
* #### **Lock Your Aadhaar:** Use the myAadhaar app or UIDAI website to "*lock*" your biometric data. This prevents anyone from using your thumbprint to make unauthorized transactions via the Aadhaar Enabled Payment System (AePS).
* #### **Check "Education Grant" Links:** Be wary of WhatsApp messages offering "*Free Laptops*" or "*Central Government Scheme*" links. Check the URL—official Indian government sites always end in .gov.in or .nic.in.
